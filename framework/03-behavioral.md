# Behavioral — applying CLEAR (as STAR)

CLEAR compresses for behavioral rounds since there's no code/design to build.

- **Clarify (~1 min)**: repeat back what's actually being asked. "Tell me
  about a conflict" has many valid stories — pick one and say which angle
  you're answering ("I'll talk about a technical disagreement with a peer,
  not a management conflict, unless you want a different kind").
- **Execute = STAR body (~4 min)**:
  - **Situation**: 2-3 sentences, just enough context to make the stakes
    legible. Do not narrate the whole project history.
  - **Task**: what specifically was your responsibility (not the team's).
  - **Action**: the bulk of the answer. Use "I," not "we," for your actual
    contribution. Narrate the decision you made and why, not just what
    happened.
  - This *is* the narration phase — same rule as coding Execute: don't go
    vague or jump straight to the outcome.
- **Assess = Result (~1 min)**: quantify if possible. If it went badly, say
  so plainly — a story where something failed and you learned something
  concrete is stronger than a manufactured success.
- **Refine = Reflection (~1 min)**: what you'd do differently now. This is
  the line senior interviewers listen for most — it signals whether you
  actually learned from the experience or are just reporting it.

## Flags specific to behavioral rounds
- **Team-speak dodge**: answering every question in "we" and never landing
  on what *you* did. Interviewers will re-ask "what did *you* specifically
  do" if this happens — practice catching it yourself first.
- **No conflict/failure stories prepared**: if every story is a clean win,
  it reads as either inexperience or dishonesty. Keep at least one real
  failure story rehearsed.
- **Rambling Situation**: if Situation takes longer than Action, that's
  backwards — tighten it.

## Story bank to prepare (fill in your own before sessions)
Keep 5-6 real stories ready, each taggable to multiple questions:
1. A time you disagreed with a decision (technical or otherwise).
2. A time you failed or shipped something wrong.
3. A time you had to influence without authority.
4. A time you handled ambiguous/incomplete requirements.
5. A time you gave or received hard feedback.
6. A time you were under significant time pressure.
