# Voice Self-Review Checklist

Claude can't hear you. This is the 60-second step that closes the gap after
every text session — do it before running `commit-session.sh`.

## How
1. Pick 2-3 of your written responses from today's session (ideally the
   Lay out and Assess phases — that's where hedging and silence show up
   most).
2. Read them aloud, at the pace you'd actually use in an interview, phone
   recorder running.
3. Play it back once. Fill in the `## Voice Self-Review` section of today's
   session file using the prompts below.

## What to listen for
- **Pace** — rushing through the important trade-off sentence, or
  dragging out the easy parts? Interviewers weight the trade-off sentence
  most; don't bury it.
- **Filler words** — "um," "like," "so yeah," "kind of." Count them if you
  can stand to. A downward trend week over week is the actual metric.
- **Where you'd go quiet** — reading your own written answer, is there a
  spot where speaking it aloud would leave a silent gap while you think of
  the next word? That's exactly where live-interview silence will happen.
  Mark it and rehearse a bridge phrase ("let me think through that for a
  second") for next time.
- **Confidence markers** — does your recorded voice trail off at the end of
  sentences (uptalk on statements, not questions)? Text can't show this;
  your ear can.

## Weekly check (every 5-7 sessions)
Re-read your day-1 `## Voice Self-Review` notes against your most recent
one. You're looking for the filler-word count and hedge count trending
down, and phase signposting becoming automatic rather than effortful. This
comparison is also the evidence you'd show someone asking "how do you know
you improved."
